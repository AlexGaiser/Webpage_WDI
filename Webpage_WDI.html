Hello!

My name is Alex Gaiser and it is a pleasure to meet you.

Let me tell you a bit about myself. I joined the Peace Corps after graduating Carroll College with a degree in History and Biology. As a Peace Corps Volunteer in El Salvador I managed projects in violence prevention, education, and youth development. A year into my service, the Peace Corps evacuated El Salvador due to concerns for volunteer safety stemming from escalating gang violence. I chose to fly back to El Salvador and finish the projects I had started, finding a job with Habitat for Humanity. I worked with Habitat for Humanity in resource development for another year before being offered a position at Cristosal, a human rights organization fighting to prosecute the Salvadoran government for war crimes committed during the civil war.

As I worked abroad, I became increasingly interested in the power that technology and software have to create real, positive change. I began writing basic scripts and programs to improve efficiency at my job and for fun. Eventually, I realized that I was more excited and fulfilled writing code in my free time than I was in the work I did professionally. With these insights, I decided to pursue a career in programming full time. I moved back to the United States and began aggressively expanding my coding skillset, working mainly with python and C#. In addition to my expanding skills as a developer, over my career I have worked hard to polish my communication, project management, and organizational abilities. As a self-taught, self-starter who is passionate about working with technology, I am excited by the opportunity work with a team to leverage my unique experience, drive and focus to tackle the difficult and diverse challenges of developing software.

-Alex
